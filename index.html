Bill Gates

Co-founder of Microsoft, Software Engineer, Investor, Philanthropist, Entrepeneur.

Bill Gates presenting a TED talk focusing on renewable energy and emission reduction

  • 1955 - Bill Gates (William Henry Gates) is born in Seattle, Washington. United States.
  • 1967 - Gates is enrolled at Lakeside school, a high-class private school in Seattle. It was here he met Paul Allen, whom would later go on to be the co-founder of Micro-soft alongside Gates.
  • 1973 - Gates enrolls in Harvard University as a Pre-Law major, but soon branches off to Advanced Mathematics and Graduate Computer Science courses.
  • 1975 - Gates contacts MITS, who were the producers of the first Personal Computer (PC) The MITS Altair in order to negotiate writing software for the device. After negotiation, MITS accepts, buying the developed language for $3,0000 USD plus royalties. At this time, Gates also took his first leave of abscence from Harvard University in order to work on Micro-soft, though it was not yet officially established.
  • 1976 - Gates and Allen register "Microsoft" as a trade mark as co-founders and Gates composes an open letter to computer hobbyists abhored that people were sharing the software rather than purchasing it.
  • 1977 - Gates takes a second leave of absence from Harvard and establishes Microsoft in Aluquerque, New Mexico. Close to MITS HQ
  • 1978 - Microsoft yearly sales exceed $1 million
  • 1979 - Microsoft HQ is relocated to Bellevue, Washington
  • 1981 - Microsoft purchases the rights to the DOS operating system from Seattle Computer Products, which was then modified and re-branded as MS-DOS before being licensed to IBM for their new personal computer the IBM 5150
  • 1983 - Allen leaves Microsoft as a result of developing Hodgkins Disease. Microsoft announces an extension to the MS-DOS operating system named Windows.
  • 1986 - Microsoft HQ reloactes to Redmond, Washington. and goes public shortly after ($21USD a share)
  • 1987 - Gates becomes the world youngest billionaire aged 31 and shortly there after meets his future wife Melinda French at an event hosted by Microsoft in New York
  • 1989 - Gates founds Corbis, which served as an archive for art and photography from public and private collections alike, later Corbis would grow to be one of the largest collections of Visual Content in the world.
  • 1994 - Gates Marries French. They consolidate their charity work under the William H. Gates Foundation, after Gate's father whom manages the foundation at its outset.
  • 1995 - Gates becomes the world's richest man at a value of 12.9 billion USD, Microsoft releases Internet Explorer along side Windows 95. Gates releases a book, 'The Road Ahead' envisioning the digital future. Microsoft's business directon acclimates to the emerging internet market.
  • 1997 - Bill and Melinda Gates launch the Gates Library foundation with an intent to help all U.S libraries have internet access for free. It is during this year that Bill goes to India for the first time to administer oral polio vaccines to children and expresses the potential of a world without polio.
  • 1998 - The Bill and Melinda Gates Children's Vaccine Program (BMGCV) is established with a gift of $100 million USD at Seattle's Program for Appropriate Technology in Health campus.
  • 1999 - Gates published his second book, 'Business @ the Speed' of Thought which focuses on the interconnections of business and technology, it was this year in which he begun to become more prevelant in pop-culture. The BMGCV program joins the Global Alliance for Vaccines and Immunizations (GAVI) who focus on enabling better accessability to vaccines and immunizations. During this time the Gates Millenium Scholar (GMS) Program is formed to assist minority students to graduate in advanced degrees in science and other disciplines.
  • 2000 - Gates steps down as the CEO of Microsoft and takes a new role as the Chief Software Architect. Additionally the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation (BMGF) is formed, absorbing several other projects and foundations including the William H. Gates foundation and the Gates Library Foundation. Many more new projects and charitable ventures are created as a result of the establishment of this foundation.
  • 2005 - Gates is knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his contributions to the United Kingdom. He is also Times 'Person of the year', sharing the title with his wife and U2 front-man, philanthropist and activist Bono.
  • 2006 - Gates announces that his role will be phased out at Microsoft and he intends to bolster his work inside of the BMGF
  • 2008 - Gates retired from day-to-day operations at Microsoft but remains as a chairman and advisor to the board. He announces his intent to pour as much into his operations as part of the BMGF as he did Microsoft.
